Fluid Momentum Study

Experimental analysis of fluid jet forces on angled targets compared to theoretical models

Project Overview

This Fluid Momentum Study was conducted by measuring forces on targets angled at 65° and 120° across a range of volume flow rates (3.0 to 7.8 L/min), comparing these to theoretical models. The experimental setup involved directing a fluid jet at the targets and recording forces using a force sensor, while modeled forces were derived from momentum conservation equations.

I analyzed the data by plotting force versus flow rate, nozzle diameter, and target angle, and performed a power-law fit, yielding exponents of 2.127 (65°, r² = 0.9991) and 2.188 (120°, r² = 0.9879), slightly above the expected value of 2. This deviation suggested a stronger flow rate dependence, potentially indicating model limitations at higher angles or flow rates.

The project enhanced my skills in experimental design, data analysis, and fluid dynamics, while highlighting the importance of reconciling theoretical predictions with real-world measurements.

Data Analysis

65° Target Angle

Measured forces align closely with modeled values at 65°, with slight deviations at higher flow rates (Power-law: F=5.995e7×V²¹²⁷, r²=0.9991)

120° Target Angle

Larger deviations observed at 120° (Power-law: F=6.123e7×V²¹⁸⁸, r²=0.9879), suggesting potential model limitations at higher angles
