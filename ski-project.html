Project Overview

The goal of this project was to design and fabricate high-performance touring skis by applying mechanical engineering principles in materials, design, and manufacturing. Using CAD and material testing, I modeled and replicated the stiffness profile of an existing ski to achieve the desired flex pattern while reducing weight.

Through iterative prototyping and testing, I refined the wood core geometry, achieving a 65% stiffness reduction with a corresponding 65% decrease in ski weight. This process allowed me to optimize performance while maintaining structural integrity.

This project deepened my understanding of structural mechanics, composites, and manufacturing, while combining engineering precision with my passion for skiing. The skis were successfully tested in various snow conditions and demonstrated superior performance characteristics compared to off-the-shelf alternatives.
